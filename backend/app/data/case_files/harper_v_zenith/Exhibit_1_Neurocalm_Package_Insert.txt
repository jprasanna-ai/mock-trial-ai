HIGHLIGHTS OF PRESCRIBING INFORMATION

INDICATIONS AND USAGE
Neurocalm is indicated for the treatment of generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) 
in adults.

DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION
- Recommended starting dose: 25 mg once daily
- May increase to 50 mg once daily after 2 weeks
- Maximum recommended dose: 75 mg once daily

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS
- Suicidal thoughts and behaviors in adolescents and young adults
- Serotonin syndrome when used with other serotonergic drugs
- Hepatic impairment: Use with caution
- Discontinuation syndrome: Taper gradually

ADVERSE REACTIONS
Most common adverse reactions (incidence ≥ 5%):
- Headache (12%)
- Nausea (9%)
- Dizziness (7%)
- Insomnia (6%)
- Dry mouth (5%)

DRUG INTERACTIONS
- MAO inhibitors: Contraindicated
- CYP3A4 inhibitors: May increase Neurocalm levels
- CNS depressants: Enhanced sedative effect

USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS
- Pregnancy: Category C
- Nursing mothers: Caution advised
- Pediatric use: Safety not established under 18 years
- Geriatric use: No dose adjustment required

CLINICAL STUDIES
Neurocalm was studied in two 8-week, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled 
trials involving 847 patients with GAD. Neurocalm demonstrated statistically 
significant improvement compared to placebo on the Hamilton Anxiety Scale.
