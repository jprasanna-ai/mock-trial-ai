JURY INSTRUCTIONS
STATE OF MIDLANDS v. JORDAN AVERY
Case No. 2024-CR-1847

INSTRUCTION NO. 1 - PRESUMPTION OF INNOCENCE

The defendant is presumed innocent. This presumption requires that the State 
prove each element of the charge beyond a reasonable doubt. The defendant is 
not required to prove innocence or present any evidence.

INSTRUCTION NO. 2 - BURDEN OF PROOF

The State has the burden of proving the defendant guilty beyond a reasonable 
doubt. This burden never shifts to the defendant. "Beyond a reasonable doubt" 
means proof that leaves you firmly convinced of the defendant's guilt.

INSTRUCTION NO. 3 - SECOND DEGREE MURDER (§ 187.2)

To find the defendant guilty of Second Degree Murder, the State must prove 
beyond a reasonable doubt:

1. That Taylor Morgan is dead;
2. That the defendant caused the death of Taylor Morgan;
3. That the defendant acted with malice aforethought; AND
4. That the killing was unlawful (not justified or excused).

"Malice aforethought" means:
- The defendant intended to kill; OR
- The defendant intended to cause serious bodily harm; OR
- The defendant acted with a conscious disregard for human life.

INSTRUCTION NO. 4 - CIRCUMSTANTIAL EVIDENCE

Evidence may be direct or circumstantial. Circumstantial evidence is evidence 
that requires an inference to connect it to a conclusion of fact. The law 
makes no distinction between the weight to be given to either direct or 
circumstantial evidence.

INSTRUCTION NO. 5 - WITNESS CREDIBILITY

You are the sole judges of the credibility of witnesses. You may consider:
- The witness's opportunity to observe
- The witness's ability to remember and describe events
- The witness's manner while testifying
- Any interest, bias, or prejudice the witness may have
- The reasonableness of the witness's testimony
