AFFIDAVIT OF DETECTIVE SAM CHEN
===============================

STATE OF MIDLANDS )
                  ) ss.
COUNTY OF RIVERSIDE )

I, Sam Chen, being duly sworn, do hereby state:

1. I am a detective with the Midlands Police Department, where I have served for 
   fifteen years. I am currently assigned to the Homicide Division.

2. On January 15, 2024, at approximately 9:47 PM, I was dispatched to the Riverside 
   Community Center regarding a reported death.

3. Upon arrival, I observed the body of the victim, later identified as Taylor Morgan, 
   lying on the floor of the main event hall near the stage area.

4. The victim displayed visible trauma to the head consistent with blunt force impact.

5. I secured the crime scene and began interviewing witnesses present at the charity 
   event.

6. Multiple witnesses identified the defendant, Jordan Avery, as having been in a 
   heated verbal altercation with the victim approximately 30 minutes before the 
   victim was found.

7. Security footage reviewed shows the defendant approaching the victim near the 
   stage area at 9:15 PM. The footage becomes obscured due to camera angle but 
   shows only the defendant and victim in that area.

8. The defendant was located on the premises and detained for questioning.

9. During questioning, the defendant admitted to having an argument with the victim 
   but denied any physical contact.

10. Based on my investigation, I believe there is probable cause to charge Jordan 
    Avery with the murder of Taylor Morgan.

FURTHER AFFIANT SAYETH NOT.
